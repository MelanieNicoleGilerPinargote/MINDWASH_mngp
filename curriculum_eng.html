I obtained my Master's degree in Architecture Construction City at Politecnico di Torino, with a thesis on parametric processes applied to urban design, mentioned as a Meritorious Thesis and awarded within the TeleArchitettura Awards. The research involved the development of an evolutionary algorithm for the generation of meta-design scenarios, applied to the case study of the former Thyssen area in Turin.

During my academic and post-graduate path, I gained advanced skills in BIM and computational design, combining research activity with tutoring experiences in parametric design studios at Politecnico di Torino, supporting students in the development of generative workflows, parametric models, and advanced project representation.

In the professional field, I participated in public and private projects and collaborated with the Research and Development department of an engineering company, dealing with the optimization of design processes and the development of computational tools for hospital design and simulation, also in support of public tenders.

Curious and innovation-oriented, I integrate design and programming as an integral part of the design process, an approach I also applied in developing my personal website as a dynamic platform for the presentation of my portfolio and curriculum. My goal is to combine architecture and technology to address the complexity of contemporary design through adaptive, sustainable processes oriented towards possible future scenarios.
